The trap to avoid: volume that hurts CTR instead of helping it

Your own GSC data already showed this pattern once: Odisha (55 pages, fully enriched) out-converts Karnataka (71 pages, less enriched) despite fewer pages, and several of your current underperformers (MMVY, e-Kalyan, SVMCM) look exactly like pages published at the "stub" completeness level this lead sheet is full of — technically live, ranking fine, but converting at 0.1-0.4% CTR because the title/content formula wasn't applied. Scaling to 500 by publishing 250 more pages at that same thin level would hit the number and quietly drag your average CTR down at the same time.

Minimum bar for anything you publish going forward

  • Title leads with whatever term students actually search (abbreviation-first where one exists) + year + "Apply Online" + "Last Date" — the exact formula validated in the CTR audit, baked in at creation instead of fixed later
  • Amount stated consistently in title and body (the Tata Pankh ₹12K vs ₹1L mismatch is the cautionary example — don't repeat it)
  • No guessed fields — "Check portal" is fine, a wrong number isn't (this is already your rule, just worth re-stating as a scaling-era guardrail)
  • Internal link from the relevant state hub page + at least one related scholarship, from day one — don't let new pages launch as orphans
