Why this matters more than just scholarship count: your own Atlas doc's Decision Log has an entry from March 2026 — "Split SC/ST/OBC post matric into separate entries — students search by their category" — and you already did this for other states. UP's current entries lumping "General, OBC, SC, ST, EWS" together into one page per level looks like it's not following your own rule. Splitting these correctly by department would likely take UP from 4 pages to potentially 9-12 (3 categories × 3-4 levels), each more precisely matched to how a specific student actually searches ("OBC scholarship UP" vs a generic page covering five categories at once) — which is also a CTR fix, not just a volume one.

⚠️ A trap worth knowing about: "Kanya Vidya Dhan Yojana"

This name shows up repeatedly across scholarship blogs as an active 2026 UP scheme (₹20,000-30,000 for girls after Class 12). Worth flagging clearly: one credible source states this scheme was launched under a previous UP government (Akhilesh Yadav) and was not continued by the current government, which runs Kanya Sumangala Yojana instead — the scheme you already correctly have live. Most of the pages still promoting "Kanya Vidya Dhan" as active appear to be low-quality SEO content recycling old information without checking current status, several with no verifiable government source and inconsistent amounts (₹20,000 vs ₹30,000 vs one very old 2017-dated figure).

Recommendation: do not add this without a direct, current, official confirmation. It's a good real-world example of exactly the trust risk your own Atlas notes call out — publishing this if it's actually defunct would be a "Verified for 2026" badge sitting on a dead scheme, which is worse than not having the page at all.
