1 Rewrite titles/meta for the CTR outliers

Est. upside: largest single lever on the report — no new content, no ranking work needed, just copy changes in your WordPress CMS fields.

What I found by actually checking three of these pages

There's a real, repeatable pattern here — not a vague "improve your titles" note. Three schemes on your underperformer list are known primarily by an abbreviation (e-Kalyan, MMVY, SVMCM), and in every case I checked, your title leads with the long official name and buries the abbreviation in parentheses. Meanwhile the dominant search query is just the bare abbreviation. Google shows your page fine (positions 4–4.5) — people just don't recognize it as the answer fast enough to click.

Jharkhand e-Kalyan VERIFIED
Dominant query "e kalyan" alone = 93,262 impressions, position 4.17, but only 0.11% CTR (100 clicks). That's the single biggest CTR gap on your whole site in absolute impression terms.

Try instead

Leads with the exact term people type, adds "2026" + "apply online" + "last date" which are the phrases converting at 2–6% CTR everywhere else on your site (see Nabanna/Kanya Utthan below). Also: three dedicated competitor sites (ekalyanscholarship.in, ekalyanjharkhand.info) are winning this click purely on name-match trust — your title needs to look at least as directly relevant at a glance.

2 Audit tata-capital-pankh-scholarship specifically

Biggest single opportunity by raw impressions (556K) — but the fix here is different from #1.

I checked this one — the title is actually already good

Current title (verified)
Tata Capital Pankh Scholarship 2026: ₹12,000 | Eligibility, Last Date & Apply

This already has 2026, an amount, eligibility, last date, apply — the exact formula from #1. Yet CTR is 1.45%, well below what that formula usually earns you. Two things stand out from what I found:

Issue A: The amount in your title may look wrong

Your title says ₹12,000, but your own page body (per the search snippet) says "Amount: ₹100000/year". A student scanning results sees a low headline number, and if they've already seen other listings quoting "up to ₹1 Lakh" (which most competitors do — Leverage Edu, ScholarshipsX, Sakshi Education all lead with the ₹1,00,000 figure), your ₹12,000 headline looks like the weakest offer in the results, even though it's really describing the lower end of a tiered amount. This is a trust/attractiveness issue, not a ranking one.

Try instead
Tata Capital Pankh Scholarship 2026: Up to ₹1 Lakh | Eligibility & Last Date

Issue B: You're in an unusually crowded SERP

This scholarship has at least 8 dedicated competitor articles ranking (Buddy4Study, WeMakeScholars, LeverageEdu, ScholarshipsX, CIGMAPedia, Sakshi, Sarsa Education, Saiket) — most with near-identical "2026: Apply Online, Eligibility, Last Date & Documents" titles. When everyone's title looks the same, CTR splits thin regardless of position. Your differentiator (29-field verified data) isn't visible in the SERP at all right now — nothing in the title or meta signals "this one is more accurate/complete than the other 8."

Also worth knowing: multiple sources note the last application deadline already expired (mentioned Feb 28, 2026), yet the query volume for "last date" variants is still active. If your page doesn't clearly say "2025-26 cycle closed — here's when the next cycle typically opens" students searching for status will bounce straight back to Google, which can quietly hurt your ranking over time even if impressions stay high.
  • Fix the title amount inconsistency (₹12,000 → ₹1 Lakh, or clarify tiers)5-minute CMS edit, directly addresses a trust gap visible in the SERP snippet itself.
  • Add a visible "Verified 29-field / last-checked [date]" trust signal to the meta descriptionYour actual differentiator vs. the 8 competing listings — right now it's invisible to searchers.
  • Add explicit cycle-status messaging if the current round is closed"Applications for 2025-26 closed Feb 28 — 2026-27 cycle typically opens [month]" keeps status-checkers on page instead of pogo-sticking back to Google.

3 Build 2-3 more "anchor" pages deliberately

This is the highest-effort, highest-payoff item — treat it as the one "big" task in your 5-slot WIP.

What makes PM Yashasvi and Sitaram Jindal different isn't just the base query

Look at how many variant queries each anchor page captures at near-top position — this is the real signature of an anchor page, and it's measurable:

Both pages rank at position 1.5–3 for a whole cluster of long-tail variants, not just the base term. That's what a strong single page with comprehensive on-page content, internal links, and (likely) some external citations/backlinks looks like. It's not luck — it's what happens when a page is treated as the definitive answer for that scholarship rather than one of many.

Good news: you already have two more pages showing early anchor behavior

Nabanna (West Bengal) and Kanya Utthan (Bihar) both already rank position 3–5 for "2026", "last date", and "apply online" variants — they're not full anchors yet, but they're the closest thing you have to a "next PM Yashasvi." That makes them your best investment target, not a totally new page.

Recommended sequence (pick 2, not all 4)

1 Nabanna — 226K impressions, already showing anchor-pattern variants. Push it over the line first.
2 Kanya Utthan — 210K impressions, same early pattern, Bihar's a priority state on your roadmap anyway.
3 Tata Capital Pankh — highest impression ceiling (556K) but crowded SERP means more work for less certain payoff.
4 HDFC Parivartan — decent signal (4.69% CTR on the "2026-27" variant) but lower total volume.

What "anchor treatment" concretely means

  • Expand the page to explicitly answer every high-volume variant as its own section/FAQ (last date, apply online, amount, eligibility, status check)
  • Add a visible "last updated" date and update it when anything changes — freshness signals matter for scholarship content
  • Internal-link to it from 3-4 related pages (state hub, category page, similar scholarships) using varied anchor text matching the query variants
  • If you have any legitimate citation opportunity (a forum answer, a resource list, a partner mention), point it here first
