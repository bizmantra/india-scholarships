## What is the Minority Merit-cum-Means Scholarship?

The Ministry of Minority Affairs (MoMA) runs the Merit-cum-Means (MCM) scholarship.
It provides financial aid to students from minority communities in India.
Eligible communities include Muslim, Christian, Sikh, Jain, Buddhist, and Parsi.

This scheme covers professional and technical courses like B.Tech, MBBS, and MBA.
Students get money to pay tuition fees and maintenance expenses.
You can read full rules on the [Merit-cum-Means Scholarship for Professional Courses](/scholarships/merit-cum-means-scholarship-for-professional-and-technical-courses) page.

> 💡 **Key Fact**: Only two students from one family can get this scholarship.

---

## Who Can Apply for Minority MCM?

You must belong to one of six notified minority communities.
You must secure at least 50% marks in your previous exam.
Your total family annual income must be under ₹2.5 Lakh.

You must study a recognized professional or technical degree course.
Admission should be through a competitive entrance exam or merit.
Distance learning and correspondence course students cannot get this grant.

Students in general BA, BSc, or BCom courses should check another option.
They can apply for the [Post-Matric Scholarship Scheme for Minorities](/scholarships/post-matric-scholarships-scheme-for-minorities) instead.
You can find more category schemes on our [Minority Scholarships Page](/scholarships-by-category/minority).

## How Much Money Do You Get?

The government pays ₹20,000 per year as maintenance allowance for hostellers.
Day scholars receive ₹10,000 per year for maintenance.
Selected premier institutes get full course tuition fee reimbursement.

Non-premier college students get tuition fee support up to ₹20,000 per year.
Payments are sent directly to your bank account via Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT).

## Step-by-Step Application Guide on NSP Portal

Follow these simple steps to register on the National Scholarship Portal (NSP).

### Step 1: Complete One-Time Registration (OTR)
Visit the official NSP website at scholarships.gov.in.
Download the NSP OTR mobile app for face authentication.
Finish eKYC to generate your unique OTR number.

### Step 2: Fill the MCM Application Form
Log in to NSP using your new OTR credentials.
Select the Merit-cum-Means Scholarship for Minorities scheme.
Enter your personal, academic, and bank account details correctly.

### Step 3: Upload Documents and Submit
Upload your minority self-declaration and income certificate.
Attach your marksheet, admission receipt, and Aadhaar card.
Submit the online form and print the receipt for college verification.

## ⚠️ 3 Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Unlinked Bank Account**: Your bank account must be seeded with Aadhaar via NPCI.
- **Income Proof Expiry**: Submit a valid income certificate issued by a Tahsildar.
- **Wrong Course Category**: General degree students must not select MCM technical scheme.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Q1: Is there an entrance exam for MCM scholarship?
No separate exam is conducted for this scholarship.
Selection relies on your class 12 or graduation marks and income.

### Q2: What is the income limit for Minority MCM?
Your total family income must be under ₹2.5 Lakh per year.

### Q3: Can two brothers apply for MCM scholarship?
Yes, up to two children from one family can apply.
