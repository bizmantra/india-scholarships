## What is the EWS Income Certificate Limit?

Economically Weaker Section (EWS) certificates help General category students get educational reservation and financial aid.

However, students often get confused between two standard limits: ₹2.5 Lakh and ₹8 Lakh per year.

Understanding this difference is critical before you submit your scholarship application form.

---

## Why are there Two Different EWS Limits?

The central government sets the main EWS reservation income ceiling at ₹8 Lakh per year.

This ₹8 Lakh limit applies to college reservation seats and entrance exams like NEET, JEE, or UPSC.

However, post-matric scholarship schemes and corporate CSR programs often enforce a stricter limit of ₹2.5 Lakh per year.

Scholarship providers prioritize lower income tiers to ensure aid reaches needy students first.

## Key Differences: ₹2.5 Lakh vs ₹8 Lakh Cutoffs

| Feature | ₹2.5 Lakh Limit | ₹8 Lakh Limit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Primary Focus** | Merit-cum-means scholarships and state fee waivers | Central EWS seat quotas and exams |
| **Target Schemes** | State welfare schemes and CSR scholarships | Govt college admissions & jobs |
| **Asset Check** | Strict check on land and house size | Standard central EWS asset criteria |
| **Eligible Examples** | State post-matric schemes, [/scholarships/tata-capital-pankh-scholarship](/scholarships/tata-capital-pankh-scholarship) | Central university admission quotas |

## How State Schemes & Corporate CSR Use Income Thresholds

CSR scholarship programs like [/scholarships/sbi-platinum-jubilee-asha-scholarship](/scholarships/sbi-platinum-jubilee-asha-scholarship) prioritize the poorest students.

Even if you hold an official ₹8 Lakh EWS certificate, private trusts may reject candidates earning above ₹2.5 Lakh or ₹3 Lakh.

Many state governments also apply tiered benefits based on income brackets:
- **Income under ₹1 Lakh**: Eligible for 100% tuition fee waiver and monthly maintenance allowance.
- **Income ₹1 Lakh to ₹2.5 Lakh**: Eligible for full tuition fee reimbursement.
- **Income ₹2.5 Lakh to ₹8 Lakh**: Eligible for 50% tuition reimbursement or admission reservation quota only.

Always read the specific guidelines of each scheme before uploading your documents.

---

## Steps to Get a Valid EWS / Income Certificate

1. **Obtain the Standard Application Form**: Download the revenue department income certificate form from your state portal.
2. **Attach Supporting Financial Proofs**: Include salary slips, IT returns, or agriculture land revenue receipts.
3. **Submit to Local Revenue Officer**: Submit your form to the Tehsildar, Circle Officer (CO), or Sub-Divisional Magistrate (SDM).
4. **Verification & Issuance**: Field verification takes 7 to 14 working days before digital signature issuance.

## Summary Checklist for EWS Applicants

1. **Verify the scheme rule**: Confirm if the limit is ₹2.5L or ₹8L.
2. **Check certificate validity**: Ensure your income certificate is issued for the current financial year.
3. **Keep digital copies ready**: Upload clear PDF copies under 200 KB to prevent portal rejection.
