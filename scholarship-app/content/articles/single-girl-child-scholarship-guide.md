## What is a Single Girl Child Scholarship?

Single girl child scholarships support families with only one daughter.
The Indian government and boards provide money for her education.
This aid encourages higher education for girl students across India.

If you have no brothers or sisters, you can apply.
Twin daughters are also eligible under specific scheme rules.
Funds arrive directly in your bank account through Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT).

---

## Top Single Girl Child Scholarships in India

India offers major government schemes for single girl children.

### 1. CBSE Single Girl Child Scholarship
The Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE) runs this merit scheme.
Girls passing Class 10 with 60% marks get aid for Class 11 and 12.
Check details on the [CBSE Merit Scholarship for Single Girl Child](/scholarships/cbse-merit-scholarship-for-single-girl-child).

### 2. AICTE Pragati Scholarship for Girl Students
The All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE) offers ₹50,000 yearly.
It supports girls in B.Tech, Engineering, and Diploma technical courses.
Explore eligibility on the [AICTE Pragati Scholarship for Girl Students](/scholarships/aicte-pragati-scholarship-for-girl-students).

### 3. UGC Indira Gandhi Single Girl Child Scholarship
The University Grants Commission (UGC) provides ₹36,200 per year.
It covers female students enrolled in first-year Post-Graduate master courses.

You can view all female schemes on our [Scholarships for Girls Hub](/scholarships-by-category/girls).

## Eligibility Criteria for Applicants

To receive single girl child financial aid, verify these general rules:

1. **Only Child Status**: You must be the only child of your parents.
2. **Twin Girls Rule**: If twin girls are born, both daughters can apply.
3. **Academic Score**: CBSE requires at least 60% marks in Class 10.
4. **Course Type**: You must study in a recognized school, college, or university.

You can verify your qualification status with our [Eligibility Checker Tool](/eligibility-checker).

## Standard Single Girl Child Affidavit Format

Every application requires an official affidavit on non-judicial stamp paper.
You must get it signed by a Sub-Divisional Magistrate (SDM) or First Class Magistrate.

### Sample Affidavit Text Template:

```text
BEFORE THE FIRST CLASS MAGISTRATE / SUB-DIVISIONAL MAGISTRATE

AFFIDAVIT

Affidavit declaration stating parent name, son/daughter of grandfather name, 
residing at [Full Home Address], do hereby solemnly affirm as under:

1. Statement of being the parent of [Girl Student Name], born on [Date of Birth].
2. That [Girl Student Name] is the ONLY child of our family.
3. That we have no other son or daughter living or deceased.
4. Confirmation that all statements made are true and correct.

Deponent (Parent Signature): _______________

VERIFICATION
Verified at [City Name] on this [Day] day of [Month, Year].
All details stated above are true and correct.

Magistrate / SDM Seal & Signature: _______________
```

> 💡 **Important Note**: Self-attested paper or simple notary stamps are often rejected. Always get your affidavit signed by an SDM or Executive Magistrate.

## Documents Required for Application

Keep scanned copies ready before filling forms on the National Scholarship Portal (NSP):

- **Single Girl Child Affidavit**: Signed by First Class Magistrate or SDM.
- **Academic Marksheets**: Class 10 or Class 12 passing certificate.
- **Bonafide Certificate**: Signed letter from school principal or college head.
- **Aadhaar Card**: Linked to mobile number for OTP verification.
- **Bank Passbook**: Active account linked with NPCI Aadhaar seeding.

## Step-by-Step Application Guide

Follow these 4 simple steps to apply online:

### Step 1: Create Account on Portal
Visit the official NSP or board portal.
Register with your Aadhaar number and active mobile phone number.

### Step 2: Select Single Girl Scheme
Log in to your dashboard.
Choose the relevant single girl scholarship scheme from the list.

### Step 3: Upload Scanned Affidavit
Fill personal, bank, and academic information.
Upload the scanned PDF copy of your Magistrate-signed affidavit.

### Step 4: Submit & Verify Form
Review all entries carefully before final submission.
Submit a printed copy with documents to your school or college clerk.

---

## ⚠️ 3 Common Errors That Reject Applications

- **Unsigned Affidavit**: Uploading a blank or non-magistrate notarized affidavit.
- **Brother/Sister Mismatch**: Submitting false family records leads to instant cancellation.
- **Bank Seeding Issue**: Account without Aadhaar linkage blocks DBT payment.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Q1: Can a girl with a brother apply for this scholarship?
No. You must be the only child of your parents to qualify.

### Q2: Are twin sisters eligible for the single girl scholarship?
Yes. Most government schemes treat twin daughters as single girl children.

### Q3: Who must sign the single girl affidavit?
A Sub-Divisional Magistrate (SDM), Tehsildar, or First Class Magistrate must sign it.
