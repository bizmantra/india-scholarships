**Important Update: Post-Matric Scholarship Applications for 2026-27**

Post-matric scholarship registrations are now open nationwide. Students should check new deadlines for the 2026-27 academic year. This follows previous extensions for the 2025-26 cycle in some states.

**National Scholarship Portal (NSP) Deadlines**

The National Scholarship Portal (NSP) opened applications on June 1, 2026. The final date for most post-matric scholarship applications is October 31, 2026. This covers schemes from various central ministries. Fresh applications began mid-July 2026. Institute verification closes around November 15, 2026. Payments are expected from December 2026 to February 2027.

**Key Eligibility**

These scholarships are for SC, ST, OBC, minority, and disabled students. Annual family income limits are generally ₹2.5 Lakh. There are plans to increase this to ₹4.5 Lakh from 2026-27.

**State-Specific Scholarship Updates**

**Telangana:** The state government will directly credit scholarships to student accounts. The ePass portal (telanganaepass.cgg.gov.in) will accept applications year-round. This applies to all eligible categories.

**Bihar:** Post Matric Scholarship (PMS) applications opened July 15, 2026. The last date to apply is September 15, 2026. Portals are pmsonline.bihar.gov.in (BC/EBC) and scstpmsonline.bihar.gov.in (SC/ST).

**Karnataka:** Registrations for 2026-27 are currently active. Deadlines vary by department and scheme. Many close by August 31 or September 30, 2026. The Social Welfare Department deadline is January 31, 2027. Apply via ssp.karnataka.gov.in.

**Odisha:** Post-matric applications for 2026-27 are expected in late November.

**Uttar Pradesh:** Pre-Matric registrations for 2026-27 are open now. Post-Matric Inter applications should open by August 2026. Check scholarship.up.gov.in for updates.

**Important Reminders for Applicants**

Always register on the official National Scholarship Portal (scholarships.gov.in). Also, check your respective state scholarship portals for local schemes. Ensure your Aadhaar number is correctly linked to your bank account for Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT). Scholarship funds will be credited directly. Timely application helps avoid last-minute issues.
